## 4. Spacetime Structures

Two events at (1,1,1,1) and (S-1,1,1,1) are 2 steps apart on a torus even though they appear at opposite ends.

**Implication:** communication across the field doesn't need to traverse the distance. It goes through the fold. Probability propagates through the shortest path on the torus — which may be "around the back."

### 4.2 Black Holes

A region of maximum convergence density. So many overlapping events that probability is 1.0 across a large volume. Properties:

- **Indestructible** — too much convergence to overcome
- **Self-sustaining** — the density generates its own resonance
- **Attractive** — nearby events are pulled into the region (their orbitals overlap and reinforce)
- **Information-rich** — every cell in the region carries influences from all contributing events

A "black hole" in the tesseract is a **truth well** — a region where so much agreement has accumulated that the state is absolute.

This is analogous to Hawking radiation: a black hole evaporates, but the information is not lost — it is encoded in the radiation. In the tesseract, a destroyed region is not lost — it is encoded in the surrounding orbitals.

### 4.4 Gravitational Lensing

A dense region bends the probability paths around it. Events near a "truth well" are influenced by its curvature — their orbitals distort toward the dense region. This creates emergent connections between events that wouldn't otherwise be related.

This is not programmed. It emerges from the geometry.
